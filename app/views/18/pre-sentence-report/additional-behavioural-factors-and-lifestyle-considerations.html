Additional behavioural factors and lifestyle considerations

Consider factors not already addressed in the report including any factors that require intervention, the person's capacity and motivation to address offending and identification of protective factors.
Help with additional behavioural factors and lifestyle considerations

Do not just describe or list all factors linked to offending as this will not provide value to the judiciary. Instead focus on factors relevant to a sentencing decision, rehabilitative needs and the defendant's current capacity and motivation to address offending.

You may want to identify and assess:

  • protective factors that may mitigate further offending
  • circumstances not directly linked to the offences that may impact desistance
  • barriers to engagement
  • caring responsibilities for children, dependants and vulnerable adults
