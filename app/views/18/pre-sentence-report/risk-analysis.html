Risk analysis

Four step process of risk assessment

This is a step-by-step process to help you make a decision about whether someone poses Low, Medium, High or Very High risk of serious harm. Follow this process by:

  • confirming risk predictors
  • analysing relevant risk and protective factors
  • assessing immediacy and likelihood of future offending
  • assigning the risk level

Is there evidence of current or past domestic abuse?

Consider the evidence of current or past domestic abuse. This includes family members and current or previous partners.

Tools such as the SARA (Spousal Assault Risk Assessment) should be completed for all individuals where there is evidence of current or past domestic abuse against a partner.

Is there evidence of current or past sexual offences?

Use the OSP (OASys Sexual Predictor) as the basis of your risk analysis for those with a current or past sexual offence. This includes sexually motivated offences.

Risk analysis

Using your understanding of the individual risk factors in this case, construct a risk analysis based on:

  • the longer-term influences (positive and negative) on the defendant's behaviour, and then the more recent influences based on professional assessment
  • the defendant's circumstances both past and present and how this may influence offending
  • the potential impact on victims
  • a decision about which RoSH (Risk of Serious Harm) level is appropriate
